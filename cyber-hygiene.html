Cyber Hygiene Checklist

Best Practices for Cyber Hygiene

Follow these practices to significantly enhance your cybersecurity posture:

  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Add an extra layer of security by requiring additional verification methods beyond just a password.
  • Regularly Update Software: Keep all your software, including operating systems and applications, up-to-date to protect against known vulnerabilities.
  • Backup Data: Regularly back up important data to ensure you can recover it in case of a ransomware attack or data loss.
  • Secure Your Network: Use firewalls and encrypt your Wi-Fi network to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Educate Yourself and Others: Stay informed about the latest cybersecurity threats and educate your family or colleagues to recognize and respond to potential threats.

Practical Exercises

Phishing Scams

How Attackers Exploit:

Attackers send emails that appear to be from legitimate sources, such as banks or companies, requesting sensitive information or login credentials.

Demo Phishing Email:


Subject: Urgent Account Verification Needed

Dear User,

We have detected unusual activity on your account. Please verify your account details by clicking the link below:

[Click Here to Verify Your Account]

Thank you,
Your Bank

Prevention Tips:

  • Be cautious of unsolicited emails asking for personal information.
  • Verify the sender’s email address and look for signs of phishing.
  • Do not click on links or download attachments from unknown sources.
  • Enable anti-phishing features in your email client.

Recovery Method:

If you suspect you’ve fallen for a phishing scam, immediately change your passwords and contact your bank or service provider. Report the incident to your IT department or local authorities if necessary.

Network Penetration Testing

How Attackers Exploit:

Attackers scan for open ports and vulnerabilities in your network to gain unauthorized access.

Demo Scan Command:

nmap -sS -p- 192.168.1.1

Prevention Tips:

  • Regularly scan your network for vulnerabilities using tools like Nmap.
  • Apply security patches and updates to your systems.
  • Use firewalls and intrusion detection systems to monitor network traffic.

Recovery Method:

If a vulnerability is discovered, immediately address it by applying patches and adjusting security settings. Conduct a thorough investigation to determine if there was any unauthorized access.

Vulnerability Assessment

How Attackers Exploit:

Attackers use tools to scan for known vulnerabilities in software or systems, which can then be exploited to gain unauthorized access.

Demo Scan Command:

openvas-cli --scan 192.168.1.1

Prevention Tips:

  • Regularly perform vulnerability assessments using tools like OpenVAS.
  • Review and apply security patches promptly.
  • Configure your systems with strong security settings and policies.

Recovery Method:

If vulnerabilities are found, take immediate action to fix them. Perform a follow-up assessment to ensure the issues are resolved and no additional vulnerabilities exist.

Trending Cyber Crimes in India

  • Phishing Scams: Phishing scams continue to rise, with attackers using increasingly convincing methods to steal personal information and login credentials.
  • Cryptocurrency Fraud: Scammers are exploiting the popularity of cryptocurrencies to perpetrate fraudulent schemes, including fake investment opportunities and Ponzi schemes.
  • Social Media Exploits: Cybercriminals are leveraging social media platforms to spread malware, conduct phishing attacks, and exploit personal information.
  • Data Breaches: High-profile data breaches are exposing sensitive information of millions of users, leading to identity theft and financial losses.
